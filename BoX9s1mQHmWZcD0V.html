Cancer in men

Hundreds of thousands of men lose the battle against cancer every year. No one is immune from cancer, but there are several ways to reduce the risk of this terrible disease just a little.

What can I do?

* Quit Smoking and limit second-hand smoke as much as possible. More men die from lung cancer than from any other type of cancer. In most cases, Smoking contributes to this.

• If you are over 50, get tested for colorectal cancer. Preventive tests can help detect this type of cancer at a very early stage, when the treatment is easier.

* Protect your skin from the sun. Skin cancer is quite common, and most cases of melanoma, the deadliest type of skin cancer, are caused by exposure to the sun's ultraviolet rays on the skin. When you are exposed to the sun for a long time, be sure to protect your skin.

• Be keep active and maintain a healthy weight. Stick to a lifestyle that includes a healthy diet and regular physical activity. This can help reduce the risk of many types of cancer.

A few facts about cancer in men.

• The most common types of cancer among men are skin cancer, breast cancer, and colorectal cancer. prostate, lung, and colorectal cancers.

* Most types of prostate cancer develop quite slowly and do not cause health problems in sick men. Therefore, it is recommended that men over 50 years of age undergo a preventive checkup.

If the cancer is detected in time, treatment is easier and gives better results. However, therapy can lead to serious side effects. Ask your doctor about the necessary examinations and possible treatment for prostate cancer.
