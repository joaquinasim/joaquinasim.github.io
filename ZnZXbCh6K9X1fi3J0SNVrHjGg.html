The main reasons for lack of adherence to treatment

Compliance, or adherence to treatment in medicine, means something quite simple - following the patient's own treatment guidelines, strict compliance with the doctor's prescriptions, implementation of its recommendations. However, it turns out that it is not easy for patients to motivate taking all their medications in the prescribed doses and deadlines. And this leads to serious problems of disease control. In General, this is not just our phenomenon, but a European and global problem. It turns out that even in countries where people are usually more likely to they are disciplined, but not all of them are strictly observed. Even in Germany, only about 60-70 percent of patients with hypertension receive treatment as prescribed.

What are the reasons for the lack of adherence to treatment?

Lack of understanding of the nature of the most common chronic diseases diseases'.

Many people do not want to admit that hypertension and diabetes, for example, still cannot be completely cured. This is not a viral infection that lasts for a week and passes. The goal of the applied therapy is to control the condition as much as possible, so that no serious complications arise. In addition, the prescribed medications should be taken regardless of what blood PRESSURE or what blood sugar level is at a particular time. An irregular pill intake is sometimes worse than a complete lack of treatment.

The opinion that if nothing hurts, then you are healthy.

It is a pity, but this is not the case. There are many examples of insidious processes, which do not cause any complaints for a long time, as long as they do not lead to significant damage to the body. For example, neither high cholesterol is a concern, nor osteoporosis (before fractures occur), nor initial and even moderate forms of kidney and liver failure. The good news is the fact that all this can be verified in laboratory tests. And the bad thing is that even after proving the pathological process, many people do not realize that they have a problem, because of which they have to take medication. And when it comes to the difficult stages, the health care system, doctors, and everyone else often become guilty...

Insufficient attention and lack of self-discipline.

After all, constantly forgetting to take vitamins at the right time and in the right dosages will then require much more time, money and effort to deal with the exacerbation of the disease. And sometimes there is no turning back...

Concerns about about the overload of the body with "chemistry".

Interesting thesis! Then we should not burden it with alcohol (C2H5OH) - pure chemistry... Seriously, the risk of drug damage when therapy is prescribed by a professional is incomparably less than the risk of an uncontrolled illness resulting in disability or death. What about protect your liver, for example, from any side effects if you develop severe atherosclerosis and stroke due to high cholesterol? It is known that the other extreme is unfavorable for the body. But this turns out to be a much rarer phenomenon. Hypochondriacs tend to be very careful about how they live, therefore, they are usually subject mainly to mental stress, and not to real illnesses.

Distrust of medicine.

Conspiracy theories can be successfully applied in any area of life - healthcare, food processing, energy, justice. In our country, this is well known to everyone. But denial the world-famous approaches to fighting diseases of the 21st century have not benefited anyone.
