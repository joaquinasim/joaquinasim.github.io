Dangerous interaction: alcohol and medications

One of the misinterpreted signs of freedom: prohibitions must be broken! To some extent, of course, the deviation from the imposed rules is a step towards progress, but with a purely personal purpose. from a practical point of view, sometimes even a small deviation from the established order can be disastrous. Especially when it comes to such a very personal and very important thing as health. Here, the range of violations of the rules is quite wide - from "I know I'm fat, but what can I do if I like it" to "The doctor forbade me to drink alcohol while taking medication, but I only drank one glass of wine."

«Only»… In fact, when it comes to risky combinations of alcohol with certain medications, this "only" can play a crucial role.

Alcohol and antibiotics.

Without question. Even one glass of wine presents a real risk of toxic liver damage, especially in people who, for example, have had hepatitis or other liver problems. Some antibiotics for the treatment of urological and gynecological infections, such as cefoperazone, cefobid, cerametal, interact with alcohol and can may cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness.

Medicines.

Their combination with alcohol is undesirable, even if it is taken in small doses. By "undesirable" here refers to the toxicity. If you have a cold and have been prescribed this medication, temporarily enroll yourself in the club of absolute teetotallers.

Aspirin.

For many people, this is the most popular remedy for the effects of a hangover. Some even take it as a preventive measure before a planned heavy drinking evening. So-definitely not! Because both alcohol and aspirin are irritating the stomach lining, and their combined action can even cause internal bleeding.

Anticoagulants.

These are medications that regulate blood clotting and prevent the formation of blood clots. The combination of them with alcohol gives an unpredictably dangerous result effect.

Antidepressants.

Their interaction with alcohol can be fatal. Depending on the type of medication and drink, the results can be very different, but always extremely unpleasant - for example, a sharp jump in blood pressure. Other combinations may have a strong sedative effect and loss of coordination.

Antihistamines.

If you have heard of them, you probably know that they are an important component of anti-allergic AIDS. But did you know that they are often part of much more common medications, for example, against the common cold. So be careful. A can of beer or a glass of wine is one thing, but if you also take a seemingly innocent pill to get rid of a stuffy nose, you will lose control of the situation. This combination significantly slows down reactions.

Barbiturates.

These drugs are used to suppress reactions of the nervous system of course, in this case, alcohol is a real Russian roulette. The result can be sad.

Anesthetics.

Anesthetics plus a little "fuel" reduce the nervous system's reactions to a critical minimum. Be careful: the described effect can be obtained even from little things.

Nitroglycerin.

cardiovascular AIDS containing it lower blood pressure. In combination with alcohol, the situation is much more complicated, and in the lightest case it can cause severe dizziness, and in severe cases-loss of consciousness.

Medicines from diabetes.

In particular, some of the tablet forms may not have the expected effect when combined with alcohol. If you are taking such medications, you should be careful.

Tranquilizers.

They by themselves slow down reactions and disrupt coordination, and even a small dose of alcohol in the blood is not enough. adding to them can lead to a disastrous result.

Extended dosage forms.

They are located in special insoluble shells, so that the active substances in them are released gradually, over many hours. But if you add alcohol, these shells will immediately dissolve. Thus, there is a risk that a dose intended to be administered in stages activates everything at once.

In General, it becomes clear - if you are taking medications, keep the bottle away from them. The only thing that has been proven is that taking b vitamins before or after a party significantly eases unpleasant feelings consequences of a hangover.
